When perceptions change, challenges become opportunity

Perceptions weave reality. It is not the supposed reality that makes us who we are but actually what we think ourselves to be. If the mind sees fear, we are frightened. If it sees sorrow, we are sad. If we can train our mind to change the perception of fear, we are no longer afraid. If we train it to change perception of sorrow to happiness, we’ll be happy.

Challenges are always misunderstood. The purpose of a challenge is not to knock us down, but to give us an opportunity to rise above our mediocre selves. So in a way, challenges are always opportunities. It is the cluttered mind that fails to benefit from this opportunity because the mind is always trapped in the wrong perception.

There are countless examples of people benefiting form change of perception:
The 32 nd US president, Franklin D Roosevelt was a man of honour who won four successive elections, despite suffering from polio since 1921. He was paralysed from the waist down, but he made sure that his people never know that, because he knew optimism was a necessity for American people in post-war America. He even trained his mind to walk with iron braces, which was extremely painful. He did not succumb to the fear of losing the challenge. He changed his perception and compelled his mind to do what was needed and hence America got its best president after Lincoln.

In Mahabharata, Karna was raised in a lower class family. His entire life he was taunted for being the son of a low born. The pathetic cast system made his life miserable but he did not give up to the then atrocious society. He wanted to become the best archer, so he changed his perception of a victim to that of an achiever and then became an archer equal in prowess to Arjuna.

There are several other examples. The zest is that a lion is a lion because its perception towards a deer is that of a hunter. A deer will never be equal to a lion because its perception is that of a fearful prey.
Reality is just a manifestation of our thoughts, challenges will always be there to keep a check that the worthy always rise above the mediocre and opportunity is equal for everyone. It is only the perception that changes person to person and only the ones who have the ability to have the right perception at the right time for the right cause will be victorious in the end.

Did we change after the outbreak?

We live in an era of organised irresponsibility. We cumulatively contribute to risks without being held individually culpable. India, reported its first COVID-19 case on January 30, 2020 and first casualty on March 12 with a total of 81 cases across the nation by second week of March. What is interesting is that the numbers and statistics no longer bother the Indian public, if it ever did. Though panic in such perilous times would be calamitous but such casual and facetious attitude and response to a pandemic is not acceptable. It seems like life after the Coronavirus outbreak hasn’t changed much. There have been changes in ‘daily-routine’ of people but no significant change in peoples’ lives. With schools and colleges shutdown, it’s just like every day is a Sunday. The very idea of seriousness of a global pandemic hasn’t yet gripped into the minds of the young population of India.

On the government’s side, measures are being taken according to the Disaster Management Act, 2005 to enhance preparedness and containment of the virus. Community awareness is being strengthened by utilizing print, electronic and social media in local languages. Local cable TV channels, phone messages, audio announcements and FM radio are being leveraged to disseminate evidence-based information on preventive measures and broadcasting helpline numbers. So the government is actively taking measures but the response of the Indian crowd is sluggish. Some may argue that there is a considerable increase in the usage of precautionary products like masks, sanitizers etc. The fact is that there is a spike in usage of such commodities but only in effected cities, towns, areas and urban centres of the nation which would not even comprise one-third of the total population. There is not a single district which is 100% aware of the situation and is taking required precautions. It is true that in a country with a population as vast as India, everything takes its own nice amount of time but time is not our friend. Though the virus is controllable if it reaches at a community spread level, it would become impossible to keep track and we will literally be at the mercy of the virus with countless deaths. Awareness is important but what is even more important is that there is dire need that people make decisions using their intellect and not their emotions. There was an incident last week when an infected Bangalore family skipped the medical desk at the airport and returned to their home which resulted in infection of four more people. Seven lives put in danger just because going home was important. It is this kind of irresponsibility that can cause mass hysteria. There were few more such cases with ten infected suspects who refused care because of their state of denial to the fact that they were facing a dangerous disease which is another major problem in general. People don’t want to accept the harsh nature of reality and they think that not responding to the change would eliminate its effects which is futile and makes all efforts made by the government go in vain.

There is no reason for fear, or unjustified acceptance that the fight is over but the need of the hour is awareness, precautions and preventions which would only come if we assimilate the fact that India is hit by a life-threating pandemic which is dangerous but controllable. We need to act and act quick. Till now there is no significant change in our lives after Coronavirus outbreak but we should be ready for unpleasant changes. We should keep in mind that maybe nothing would be same ever again but that does not mean that nothing would be good. We have to be aware and ready because it is our responsibility that this pandemic is put to an end as soon as possible.
