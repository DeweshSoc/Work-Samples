Elements of a Communication System


How are we able to exchange words over the phone?
How is a picture sent from one mobile is recieved by another one which is a thousand miles away?
Answer is that we have something called a COMMUNICATION SYSTEM.

But what is communication?
Communication is the exchange of information. The best way to understand this is talking to someone. When I say something (transmit), you listen (recieve) it and then you interpret it. This is nothing but COMMUNICATION. In a communication system our porpuse is to transmit some signal (that contains information) from one end and then recieve it at the other end, where the signal is interpreted to get the correct information.

The following is the block diagrom of a Communication System.



  • At the source, some message is originated. This message can be of any kind. For eg: human speech, SMS, pictures, vedios etc. The important point here is that this message may or may not br in electric form. So, for transmission purpose it must be converted into an electrical signal using a TRANSDUCER. The output of the transducer is an electrical signal, which is now called a BASEBAND signal or MESSAGE signal.
  • The message signal is fed to the transmitter, which modifies it for better, efficient transmission. Now this modified message signal is transmitted into the channel.
  • The CHANNEL is just a medium through which the signal flows. Eg: wires for telephones, air for cell phones, vaccum for gallactic communication. So the signal travels through the channel until it reaches the receiver.
  • The purpose of the Receiver is to process the signal received from the channel by undoing the signal modifications made at the transmitter and in the channel. So at the receiver output we have the same unmodified message signal (that was earlier fed to the transmitter).
  • The receiver output (i.e our message signal) is now fed to another transducer that convert the electrical signal to the original form.

The Transmitter, Channel and the Recevier are collectively called the three elements of communication.


This way a person speaking something in a phone can be heared by someone 1000m away. The phone has a transducer that converts the human speech (sound) to message signal (electric), which is then fed to the transmitter. Transmitter does its part and the modified signal is transmitted into the channel (wires in case of telephones).
The receiver's phone has a receiver that demodifies the signal to get back the message signal and feeds it to the transducer that converts this signal (electric) back to human speech (sound), which is heard via a speaker.
